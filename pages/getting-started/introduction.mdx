## The Problem

Standard conversion tracking (gtag.js, Facebook Pixel, UET) relies on:

1. Client-side JavaScript executing successfully
2. Cookies persisting until conversion
3. Tracking requests not being blocked

In 2024, this fails 25-40% of the time:

| Failure Mode | Impact |
|--------------|--------|
| Ad blockers | 20-30 percent of users |
| iOS Safari ITP | All iOS Safari users after 7 days |
| Cross-device | 30-40 percent of customer journeys |
| Cookie clearing | Varies by audience |

## What You Can Track

### Conversion Types

- **Purchase** - E-commerce transactions with revenue
- **Lead** - Form submissions, contact requests
- **Signup** - User registrations, trial starts
- **Subscription** - Recurring payment signups
- **Download** - Ebook, software, resource downloads
- **Custom** - Any event you define

### Data Captured

Every conversion includes:

- **Attribution** - Click ID, UTM parameters, referrer
- **Session** - Page views, time on site, device info
- **User Data** - Hashed email/phone for Enhanced Conversions
- **Value** - Revenue, currency, order details
- **Geography** - Country, city, timezone

## Platform Integrations

Convultra forwards conversions to all major ad platforms:
